About

My name is Jacob Arndt and I am a Masters student at the University of Minnesota studying geographic information science. In addition to my studies in geographic information science, I am also earning a graduate minor in computer science. Before beginning my Masters, I graduated from the University of Minnesota with a Bachelor of Science in geography and a Bachelor of Arts in music. Looking back, my time as an undergraduate student appears unfocused and I guess this might be the result of my wandering curiousity and interest in many things. I was first drawn to geography while taking a biogeography course. It was there that I discovered a passion for science and what soon became my primary interest, climatology. After declaring a major in geography, I began taking courses in GIS and quickly found myself enjoying the technical and analytical aspects of GIS.

My primary interests lie at the intersection of the climate system, spatio-temporal data, remote sensing, and GIS.
